Glossary

Every bit of jargon used across the papers, in plain English. No prior knowledge assumed.

3
3D model
A picture of the building you can rotate, zoom and click on a screen, rebuilt from the floor plan rather than a fixed photo or render.
precision
A
Acclimation
The multi-week adaptation in which a plant builds more chloroplasts, thicker leaf cuticles, and higher enzyme density so it can safely use higher light.
reference
Acclimatisation (hardening)
Gradually toughening a tissue-cultured plantlet up, lowering humidity and raising light, so it can survive in normal air.
tissue culture
Action limit
A pre-set value that, when crossed, forces a defined response (and a corrective-action note). Paired with a softer alert limit that just warns.
operationsprecision
Action threshold
A defined pest level (e.g. one pest seen, or more than 3% of a room affected) that triggers a specific control response.
reference
Advisory / prescription
An output that names the action, the setpoint, the deadline and the expected outcome - 'do this, by when, or expect that' - instead of just raising an alarm.
reference
Air embolism
An air bubble drawn into a cut stem that blocks water uptake; prevented by sticking cuttings fast and keeping cut ends wet.
reference
Air-filled porosity
The share of a medium's pore space that holds air right after draining; it trades off directly against water-holding capacity.
reference
Airlock / gowning cascade
A sealed transition room with interlocked doors where personnel escalate protective clothing before entering a higher-grade space.
reference
ALCOA+
Data-integrity rule: records must be Attributable, Legible, Contemporaneous, Original, Accurate, plus Complete, Consistent, Enduring, Available.
reference
Aliasing
When sampling too slowly folds a fast pattern into a slow one that was never really there, the wagon-wheel-spinning-backwards effect in data.
reference
Alkalinity
Water's built-in capacity to neutralise acid, mostly from bicarbonates, reported as ppm of calcium carbonate (CaCO3). Not the same as a high pH reading.
reference
Annex 11 / PI 041
GMP requirements for computerised systems and data integrity (access control, audit trails, validation, backups).
reference
Apical dominance
A plant's natural tendency to grow one central top taller than its side branches. Topping and LST break this so growth spreads evenly.
reference
AppDaemon
The Python app environment running the ~5900-line master Crop Steering decision loop on top of Home Assistant.
precision
AppDaemon engine
The autonomous brain (master_crop_steering_app.py) that reads sensors and settings, decides each row's phase, and runs the hardware sequence.
precision
Aseptic technique
Working so that no bacteria, fungi or yeast get into your culture. The single most important skill in tissue culture.
tissue culture
ASTM D8197
The industry standard specifying the 0.55-0.65 aw range for dry cannabis flower.
reference
Auto-tick (self-completing check)
A check item that a system marks done by itself when its telemetry confirms the condition, leaving the human only the items a sensor cannot judge.
operationsprecision
Autoflower seed
Seed with ruderalis genetics that flowers automatically after a few weeks regardless of light schedule.
reference
Auxin
A class of plant hormone that promotes root growth. IBA is the best one for cannabis.
tissue culturepropagation
B
Background drip baseline
The steady AC/dehumidifier drip learned at night so it can be subtracted from total drain readings.
precision
Baseline / trajectory
The expected envelope for a given cultivar at a given stage and photoperiod phase, learned from your own past runs.
precision
Batch / lot
One defined production run carrying a single ID that follows the material through every transfer.
reference
Batch / Lot Record
The document capturing all materials, actions, people, equipment, results and disposition for one batch (FRM-MF-004), consolidated in REG-CU-001.
reference
Batch release
The controlled decision, backed by test results and records, that a finished batch is safe and meets spec before it can be sold.
quality
Bicarbonate (HCO3-)
The main dissolved compound responsible for water alkalinity; it consumes added acid and pushes pH back up.
reference
Bioaerosol
Airborne particles carrying living things, such as skin flakes with bacteria, fungal spores, or droplets from speech or a toilet flush.
health
Biocontrol / beneficial
A living organism released to suppress a pest, a predatory mite, a parasitoid wasp, or a microbe such as a fungus or bacterium.
reference
Biological control (biocontrol)
Releasing predatory insects or mites that eat pests; harmless to the plant and self-limiting when prey runs out.
reference
Bleaching
Pale or white discoloration (usually at upper-canopy tips) from light/oxidative stress, the visible result of photoinhibition.
reference
Blocked-dripper guard
A fail-safe that parks a row for ~2 hours after too many failed emergency shots (default 4 in 30 min), with a per-row on/off toggle.
precision
Bti / Btk
Strains of the soil bacterium Bacillus thuringiensis, israelensis (Bti) kills fungus-gnat larvae and kurstaki (Btk) kills caterpillars, both only when the pest ingests them.
reference
Bucking
Cutting the finished buds off the stem after trimming.
reference
Bud rot (botrytis)
A grey mould that attacks dense buds in humid conditions, a major late-flower risk that drives the push to lower humidity.
reference
Bud set
The point around weeks 3-4 when scattered pistils organise into defined, growing budlets.
reference
Buffer (buffering)
A medium's natural ability to resist pH change; soil buffers well, coco and hydroponics barely buffer at all, so they need tighter pH control.
reference
Buffer solution
A liquid of precisely known pH (e.g. 4.0, 7.0) used to calibrate a pH pen.
reference
Buffering
A medium's ability to absorb your errors in pH or nutrient strength so the roots see a steadier environment.
reference
Buffering capacity
A medium or water's resistance to pH change. High buffering resists swings (soil); low buffering swings quickly (coco, hydro).
reference
Build back
The practice of re-adding minerals (usually a CalMag supplement plus base nutrients) to mineral-stripped RO or rainwater, since the plant still needs calcium and magnesium and some buffer.
reference
Bulk EC
Electrical conductivity of the entire wet-media mixture (solids, water, air), measured directly by the probe over 0-20000 microS/cm.
reference
Bulking
The mid-flower phase (roughly weeks 5-7) when buds gain mass fastest.
reference
Burping
Briefly opening a sealed container during curing to release humid air and let fresh air in.
reference
C
C1D1
Class 1 Division 1, an electrical/room classification for areas where flammable solvent vapour may be present, requiring explosion-proof equipment and gas detection.
reference
Cal-mag
A calcium and magnesium supplement; essential when running coco to prevent the deficiency caused by coco's CEC.
reference
Calibration
Adjusting a sensor's raw reading so it matches reality for your specific substrate, without it, the percentage on screen can be off.
sensors
Calm dashboard
The exception-based screen that stays quiet by default and speaks only when it has something worth saying.
precision
CalMag
A calcium-and-magnesium (often with iron) supplement added to low-mineral water like RO, rain, or very soft tap to prevent calcium/magnesium deficiency.
reference
Calvin cycle
The CO2-using half of photosynthesis that turns captured light energy into sugar. If CO2 is too low, this stage cannot keep up with the light reactions.
reference
Calyx
A single teardrop-shaped floral pod; many calyxes stacking together build a bud.
reference
Cannabis ruderalis
A hardy cannabis subspecies whose genetics give autoflowers their automatic, light-independent flowering.
reference
CAPA
Corrective And Preventive Action: the formal process of fixing a problem and stopping it from happening again. A core GMP habit.
quality
CAPA (Corrective and Preventive Action)
A root-cause-driven process to fix an existing problem (corrective) and stop it recurring or occurring (preventive).
reference
Capacitance sensor
A probe that measures how much water is in the substrate by sensing its electrical 'permittivity', water changes it far more than soil or air do.
sensors
Carbonate / bicarbonate
The dissolved compounds (CO3 and HCO3) responsible for alkalinity. They neutralize added acid and can lock up calcium and magnesium when in excess.
reference
CCP (Critical Control Point)
A process step with a measured limit that prevents a specific hazard; HACCP terminology used for in-process control.
reference
CEC (cation exchange capacity)
How strongly a medium grabs and holds positively-charged nutrient ions (in meq/100g); high CEC means more buffering of feed mistakes.
reference
Change Control
The formal process for assessing, approving and implementing any change that could affect product quality or the validated state.
reference
Channeling (preferential flow)
Water sneaking straight down through the substrate, missing the roots (and often the sensor).
precision
Chloramine
A more stable chlorine-ammonia disinfectant that does NOT off-gas from standing water and requires a catalytic carbon filter or reverse osmosis to remove.
reference
Chlorine
A common municipal disinfectant that off-gasses from standing, aerated water in about 24 hours. Can damage root tips and beneficial microbes at low concentrations.
reference
Chloroplast
The cell structure that does photosynthesis; plants build more of them during acclimation to handle higher light.
reference
Chlorosis
Yellowing of leaf tissue caused by loss of green chlorophyll.
reference
Cleanroom grade
A rating for how few airborne particles a room allows. Stricter grades (and gowning) are used where product contamination must be controlled.
qualityfacility
Cleanroom grade (CNC, D, C, B, A)
A ranking of how clean a room's air is. CNC is uncontrolled; Grade A is cleanest. Mapped to ISO 14644 classes (D=ISO 8, C=ISO 7, A=ISO 5).
reference
Clone
A genetically identical copy of a plant, whether made by a cutting or by tissue culture.
propagation
Closed loop
A control system where the result feeds back to drive the next action: measure, decide, act, then measure the effect of that action, repeating continuously.
reference
Closed loop (gated)
Letting the system auto-apply only low-risk, explicitly-licensed actions, with everything irreversible or expensive kept human-approved.
precision
Closed-loop control
When a system measures a result, compares it to a goal, and adjusts itself automatically, a thermostat is the classic example.
control
CO2 supplementation
Deliberately raising carbon dioxide above ambient (~400–600 ppm) to match high light, required to run intensities like 1200 or 1500 µmol/m²/s without bleaching.
reference
CoA (Certificate of Analysis)
The document listing a batch's test results against specification, issued at release.
reference
Coco coir
A coconut-husk growing medium used instead of soil; drains fast and responds quickly to watering.
precision
Coefficient of variation (CV)
A measure of spread relative to the average; applied to a batch of plants it literally quantifies the noise (inconsistency) in your crop.
reference
Cola
A large, dense cluster of buds formed along a main stem, usually the topmost and best-developed flowers.
reference
Common cause vs special cause
Common-cause variation is normal jitter you leave alone; special-cause variation is an unusual event (a point past a limit, or a non-random run) worth investigating.
reference
Common-cause variation
The natural, expected jitter of a stable process; it lives inside the control limits and the correct response is to do nothing.
reference
Confidence
The brain's self-rated trust in its current estimate; high confidence allows watering, low confidence forces caution.
precision
Control limits
Boundaries computed from a process's own history (typically the mean ± 3 standard deviations) that separate normal variation from abnormal.
reference
Control limits (SPC)
Statistically-set lines around normal variation. A reading inside them is just noise; one outside is a real signal worth acting on. From 'statistical process control'.
control
Cotyledons
The two rounded embryonic 'seed leaves' that appear first and feed the seedling from stored reserves.
reference
Coupled system
A system where moving one control changes several others at once, so no lever acts in isolation.
reference
Crop steering
Nudging a plant toward vegetative (leafy) or generative (flower/fruit) growth by controlling irrigation, climate and light.
crop steering
Crop Steering AI (CS)
The sensor-driven 4-phase irrigation brain with per-zone control, EC logic and ML prediction; the active irrigation controller.
precision
Cross-contamination
Carrying contamination from a dirty area or item to a clean one, e.g. walking from flower back into propagation without re-gowning.
operationshealth
Culling
Sorting and removing weak or unrooted clones (keepers / rejects / kills) before transplant.
reference
Curing
Letting a finished batch settle to one even water activity while preserving terpenes and preventing mould.
reference
Cutting
A severed shoot being rooted into a clone; used interchangeably with 'clone'.
reference
Cytokinin
A class of plant hormone that promotes shoot and bud growth. Examples: meta-topolin, BAP, TDZ.
tissue culture
D
Damping-off
A fungal disease (Pythium, Fusarium, Rhizoctonia) that rots a seedling at the soil line, usually killing it.
reference
Dead-band
The tolerance window a controller allows before it acts; too narrow a dead-band makes equipment over-react and 'hunt'.
reference
Defanning / Defoliation
Selectively removing large fan leaves to open the canopy for light penetration and airflow.
reference
Deficiency
The plant is not getting enough of a nutrient, producing fading, yellowing and stunted growth.
reference
Defoliation
Selectively removing leaves to improve light penetration and airflow into the canopy.
reference
Deviation
Any departure from the approved, documented process; it must be logged and investigated.
reference
Digital twin
A 3D model connected to live sensor feeds so it displays the facility's real current temperature, humidity and equipment status.
precision
Dilution
Spreading a substance through a large volume of water so its concentration is low; the safety margin that makes adding Part B safe after Part A.
reference
Dissolved oxygen (DO)
The amount of oxygen dissolved in water, which roots need. It falls as water warms (about 5-6 ppm at 20 C down to 3-4 ppm at 26 C), and low DO encourages root disease.
reference
DLI
Daily Light Integral, the total amount of usable light a plant receives over a full day, in mol/m²/day. It combines intensity (PPFD) with hours of light, so two intensities can give the same DLI at different photoperiods.
reference
DLI (Daily Light Integral)
The total PAR photons delivered to a surface over a full day, in moles per square meter per day (mol/m2/day). DLI = PPFD x seconds of light per day / 1,000,000. It is the dose that most directly drives yield.
reference
Dome
The clear plastic humidity lid placed over a clone tray to trap moisture around rootless cuttings.
reference
Draw call
One instruction to the graphics card to draw something; fewer draw calls means a faster model (aim for under ~300).
precision
Drench
A treatment applied to the growing media and root zone rather than the foliage.
reference
Drift
A slow, one-directional sensor error that pretends to be a real trend. The dangerous form of noise; defeated only by calibration and cross-checking.
reference
Dry-to-wet ratio
Final dry weight as a fraction of starting wet weight; around 10% in the source data.
reference
Dryback
The deliberate drop in root-zone moisture between irrigations, used to steer the plant generative or vegetative.
crop steering
Dryback %
The size of a dryback, in percentage points of water content: the daily peak minus the next trough (e.g. 78% to 58% is a 20-point dryback).
crop steering
DUL (drained upper limit) / container capacity
How much water a specific container holds after free drainage stops; an observed per-pot state, not a textbook soil constant, that drifts as roots grow.
reference
DWC (deep water culture)
Hydroponics where roots hang in oxygenated nutrient water with no solid medium; oxygen comes from an air pump.
reference
E
EC
Electrical Conductivity, a measure of nutrient strength in the feed water, in mS/cm.
reference
EC (electrical conductivity)
A proxy for how much fertiliser salt is dissolved in the root zone. Higher EC = stronger feed.
crop steeringsensors
EC ratio
Measured EC divided by target EC. Above 1 = too salty (water more to dilute); below 1 = too weak (hold back).
precision
Efficacy (umol/J)
How many usable PAR photons a fixture produces per joule of electricity. The key efficiency number when comparing lights: good LEDs are ~2.7-3.0 umol/J, HPS ~1.7-1.9, CMH ~1.3-1.9.
reference
EM (Environmental Monitoring)
The program that samples non-viable particles, viable air, surfaces, and personnel against alert and action limits.
reference
Embryo
The tiny living baby plant inside the seed, already containing a root, two cotyledons, and a shoot.
reference
Endophyte
A microbe that lives inside healthy-looking plant tissue. The hardest contaminant in tissue culture because surface bleach can't reach it.
tissue culturedisease
Entomopathogenic nematodes
Microscopic worms (e.g. Steinernema feltiae) drenched into the medium to parasitise and kill fungus-gnat larvae.
reference
ESPHome
The firmware/platform that runs the physical sensors and some switches, reporting into Home Assistant.
precision
EWMA
Exponentially-weighted moving average, a rolling average that weights recent readings more heavily, giving less lag for the same smoothing.
reference
Exclusion
Limiting who and what enters an affected room to stop pests spreading between rooms.
reference
Explant
The small piece of plant you cut off and place into the culture vessel to start a new culture.
tissue culture
Extrude
To take a flat 2D shape (like a floor outline) and push it upward to give it thickness or height.
precision
F
Fail-safe read
If the engine can't confirm the master arm switch, it treats the system as disabled rather than firing, so a state-sync gap can't make it act blind.
precision
Fan leaves
The large leaves with few trichomes, removed by hand early in trimming.
reference
Far-red
Light around 700-750 nm, just past the PAR band. It is sensed by phytochrome, can speed the perceived onset of night, and adds some stem stretch; high doses can reduce bud density.
reference
Feedback
Using the measured result of an action to decide the next action. The heart of any self-correcting (closed-loop) system.
control
Feminised seed
Seed bred to produce almost only female plants, removing the need to identify and remove males.
reference
Field capacity
The maximum VWC the medium can hold before water just runs off; default cap is 70%.
precision
Field capacity / substrate EC
The salt concentration held in the root-zone medium; the Athena guide gives stage targets (e.g. 3-5 in veg) that your mixed feed is meant to build toward.
reference
FIM
A partial topping where most but not all of the growing tip is cut, which can produce several new tops rather than the two from a clean top.
reference
Fit-out
The equipment and furniture placed inside the bare shell: benches, lights, dehumidifiers, tanks, racks.
precision
Flip (12/12)
Switching the grow-light timer to 12 hours on and 12 hours off, the change that triggers flowering in photoperiod plants.
reference
Floor plan
The architect's flat, top-down drawing of walls, doors and rooms, usually dimensioned in millimetres.
precision
Florigen
The mobile flowering-signal protein made in the leaves once nights are long enough; it travels to the growing tips and reprograms them from making leaves to making buds.
reference
Flush
Running plain or lightly fertilised, correctly pH'd water through the medium to wash out accumulated salts and reset the root zone.
reference
Foliar spray
A liquid treatment applied to leaves and stems, as opposed to the root zone.
reference
Fomite
An object that carries and transfers microbes, e.g. a phone, pen, tool or handle. Hands move contamination from fomites to the crop.
health
FOV cone
A translucent 'field of view' wedge showing exactly what a camera can see; floor outside every cone is a candidate blind spot.
precision
Frass
Insect droppings and debris; an early visible sign of a pest presence during scouting.
reference
Full pot (DUL, drained upper limit)
The most water a pot can hold after it has finished draining.
precision
G
GACP
Good Agricultural and Collection Practice, the upstream cultivation/drying standard that feeds compliant biomass into the GMP facility.
reference
GACP (Good Agricultural and Collection Practice)
The cultivation and collection standard applied to medicinal-cannabis starting material, recognising it as a botanical crop before it becomes a medicine.
reference
GDP (Good Documentation Practice)
Rules for making and correcting records: contemporaneous, indelible, original preserved, corrections by a single legible line that is initialled, dated and reasoned.
reference
Genealogy / traceability
The forward-and-backward record linking any finished unit to its source lots, operators, equipment, and conditions, what makes a targeted recall possible.
reference
Generalist vs specialist predator
A generalist eats many pest types (and pollen) and persists without prey; a specialist eats one pest type fast but fades without it.
reference
Generative vs vegetative
Two growth modes: vegetative = leaves and stems and size; generative = flowers, fruit and density. Crop steering shifts the balance.
crop steering
Germination
The process of a dormant seed taking up water, waking, and pushing out its first root and shoot.
reference
Germination rate
The percentage of a batch of seeds that successfully sprout.
reference
glTF
A standard 3D file format for importing detailed models authored in tools like Blender, or exporting the building back out.
precision
GMP (good manufacturing practice)
A quality framework of rules and records that ensures products are made safely, consistently and traceably.
quality
Gowning / de-gowning
Donning and doffing protective clothing in a defined order across a clean/dirty line, so contamination never crosses. Gown top-down; de-gown in reverse, dirtiest first.
operationshealth
Grams per litre (g/L)
The dosing unit for powdered nutrients; multiply the label g/L by tank volume (e.g. x50 for a 50 L tank) to get grams to weigh.
reference
H
Hardening off
Gradually opening vents and removing the dome so rooted clones acclimate to drier room air before transplant.
reference
Hardness
The amount of dissolved calcium and magnesium in water, reported as ppm CaCO3 or grains per gallon (1 GPG = 17.1 mg/L). Soft water is under ~1 GPG; very hard is over ~10.5 GPG.
reference
Hermaphroditism (hermie)
When a stressed female plant produces male parts and pollinates itself, seeding the buds; often caused by light leaks in the dark period.
reference
Hierarchy of controls
The legal order for managing risk: eliminate, substitute, isolate, engineer, administrate, then PPE as the last line.
operations
Hilhorst model
A 2000 method to estimate pore-water EC from bulk EC, VWC and temperature; useful but ~+/-20% sensitive and invalid below VWC 0.10 m3/m3.
reference
Home Assistant
Free open-source home-automation software that runs the dashboards, automations, and integrations described here.
precision
Honeydew
Sticky sugary excretion from aphids and whitefly that coats leaves and grows black sooty mould.
reference
Hop Latent Viroid (HpLVd)
A tiny loop of RNA, smaller than a virus, that causes 'dudding' in cannabis: stunted plants, smaller buds and big losses in cannabinoids and terpenes.
disease
Humidity dome
A clear cover placed over seedlings to trap moisture and keep humidity high while roots are tiny.
reference
Hunting
Oscillation in a feedback loop that over-corrects back and forth instead of settling, often caused by noisy input or too-tight tuning.
reference
Hydroponics
Growing with roots in a nutrient water solution (or inert media fed by it) rather than soil; needs slightly acidic, closely managed pH.
reference
Hyperhydricity (vitrification)
A disorder where shoots turn glassy, swollen and water-soaked. They root and harden poorly and usually die at transplant.
tissue culture
I
ICH Q9 / Q10
International harmonisation guidelines for Quality Risk Management (Q9) and the Pharmaceutical Quality System (Q10).
reference
Imbibition
The initial swelling of a seed as it absorbs water, the first physical step of germination.
reference
Immobile nutrient
A nutrient that stays fixed where it was first deposited and cannot be relocated (Ca, S, Fe, Mn, Zn, B, Cu, Mo); its deficiency shows on the newest/top growth first.
reference
Implementation intention
An if-then plan that ties a task to a fixed time and place ('at lights-on, the AM tech does X'), which makes follow-through 2-3x more likely.
operations
In vitro
Latin for 'in glass', anything happening inside the sterile culture vessel. Opposite of ex vitro (out in the real world).
tissue culture
Indexing
Lab-testing a plant (e.g. by RT-qPCR) to confirm it is free of a specific disease. Proving a plant is clean.
tissue culturedisease
Inert medium
A substrate (like rockwool) that holds no nutrients of its own, so the plant gets exactly what you feed and nothing else.
reference
Inference layer
The part of the system that estimates things you cannot measure directly (plant stress, transpiration, vigor) from things you can.
precision
Instancing
Drawing many identical objects (like 210 plants) in one batch instead of one at a time, the main way to keep the model fast.
precision
Integrated Pest Management (IPM)
A repeating loop of evaluating, deciding, and controlling pests using the lightest effective method, escalating only as needed.
reference
Internode
The length of bare stem between two nodes.
reference
Interveinal chlorosis
Yellowing between the leaf veins while the veins themselves stay green, a specific pattern seen with magnesium, iron and manganese problems.
reference
Inverse-square law
Light intensity falls off with the square of distance: roughly doubling the fixture-to-canopy gap cuts PPFD by about 75%. This makes hanging height the coarse intensity control.
reference
IPM (Integrated Pest Management)
A layered pest strategy combining prevention, monitoring, biological controls and targeted treatments rather than routine calendar spraying. This guide identifies the pests; the IPM SOP is the decision process.
reference
ISO 14644
The international standard that classifies cleanrooms by the number of airborne particles allowed per cubic metre.
reference
J
JSON / data file
A plain, human-readable list of facts (room names, sizes, positions) that the code reads to build the model.
precision
K
KCl storage solution
Potassium chloride solution that keeps a pH probe tip hydrated; storing dry or in plain water permanently damages the probe.
reference
Kelvin (K) / color temperature
A shorthand for the color mix of a white LED. Higher Kelvin (4000-6500K) is bluer and veg-leaning; lower Kelvin (3000-3500K) is redder and flower-leaning.
reference
Killer item
A check that is both easy to skip and dangerous to miss (dehumidification at lights-off, a dosing pump with no irrigation timeout). The few items a checklist must force.
operations
L
Larf
Small, airy, underdeveloped flower from shaded lower bud sites, the low-value growth lollipopping removes.
reference
Leading vs lagging indicator
A precursor that warns early (leading) versus a symptom that confirms damage already happened (lagging).
precision
Learned envelope
The expected healthy range for a value given the cultivar, growth stage and photoperiod, used instead of one fixed threshold.
reference
Lever (actuator)
Any control you can change in the room - light intensity, cooling setpoint, irrigation shot, CO2 dosing. The paper counts eleven main lever families.
reference
Life cycle / generation time
The time from egg to egg-laying adult. In warm dry rooms this drops below a week for spider mites, which is why infestations explode.
reference
Light saturation
The point where a plant cannot use more light because CO2, water, nutrients, temperature, or genetics has become the limiting factor; beyond it, extra intensity adds cost and stress, not yield.
reference
Light stress / bleaching
Damage from too much intensity, showing as white/yellow bud tips directly under the fixture, upward-cupping 'taco' leaves, and faded color. The fix is to dim or raise the light, not to feed more.
reference
Living soil (ROLS)
A microbe-rich organic soil bed (recycled organic living soil) that self-buffers pH and feeds plants via biology; water-only and reusable indefinitely.
reference
Lockout
When nutrients become chemically unavailable to the plant, here, caused by precipitation when concentrated Part A meets Part B.
reference
Lollipopping
Removing the small branches, nodes and leaves from the lower third (roughly the bottom 10-18 inches) of the plant so energy goes to the top buds, leaving a lollipop shape.
reference
Loupe / scope
Magnifiers for inspection, a 30x hand loupe finds spider mites; russet and broad mites need 60-100x magnification.
reference
LST (Low-Stress Training)
Gently bending and tying down branches to spread the canopy flat without cutting the plant, lower stress and no recovery time.
reference
M
MACO
Maximum Allowable Carryover, the calculated residue limit used as the acceptance criterion in cleaning validation.
reference
Macronutrient
An element needed in large amounts: nitrogen (N), phosphorus (P), potassium (K), calcium (Ca), magnesium (Mg) and sulfur (S).
reference
Management Review
The periodic (at least annual) review by senior management of the quality system's performance and effectiveness.
reference
Manual override
An absolute per-zone lockout: when ON, nothing waters that zone - not auto, not emergency, not a manual trigger.
precision
Master Document Register (REG-QM-001)
The keystone index listing every controlled document with its current version, owner and review status.
reference
Median filter
Takes the middle value of the last N readings; excellent at deleting single-point spikes without smearing real step changes.
reference
Medium (media)
The food a culture grows on: a jelly of mineral salts, sugar, vitamins and (optionally) hormones, set firm with a gelling agent.
