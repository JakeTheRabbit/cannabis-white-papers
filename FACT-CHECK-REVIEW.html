Full fact-check review — Cannabis White Papers

Site reviewed: jaketherabbit.github.io/cannabis-white-papers · Corpus v1.2 (updated 2026-07-18) · 37 papers · source: papers/*.md

Scope: claims that are false, half-true, misleading, poorly worded, under-explained, or where a picture would fix confusion. Directionally solid science is not listed as “broken.” Priority: safety, numbers, citation hygiene, jurisdiction.

Method: full markdown read + cross-paper consistency + physiology/hort checks Repo: JakeTheRabbit/cannabis-white-papers Date: 2026-07-19
Overall verdict: This is a strong beginner-first, evidence-linked field guide corpus — better than most grow-blog content — but it is not peer-reviewed science as marketed. Highest-risk fixes: site branding, PPE log-math, sulphur Tbsp rates, irrigation veg/gen dryback inversion, dissolved-oxygen numbers, leaf-drinking seedling claim, ambient 800 µmol hard ceiling vs RM2021, late-flower RH band, Caplan drought mis-mapped to daily drybacks, and biosignal overclaim.
Severity key
  • FALSE — claim is wrong; rewrite or remove.
  • HALF_TRUE — directionally real but overstated / incomplete.
  • MISLEADING — wording or framing will send readers the wrong way.
  • UNCLEAR — cannot verify as stated / needs a primary source.
  • NEEDS_BETTER_EXPLAIN — not false, but beginners will misapply.
  • IMAGE_HELP — visual would prevent a common misconception.
  • SITE_WIDE — marketing / multi-paper / jurisdiction issue.
